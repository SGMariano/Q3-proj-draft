About the Artist

Bea Kristi, who was raised in London and was born in the Philippines, began composing music under the name Beabadoobee in 2017. Beabadoobee, who is only 20 years old, has amassed a major, devoted following of people thanks to her outstanding output of self-revelant bedroom pop songs and DIY style. Through a fan-uploaded video, her debut single, "Coffee," received hundreds of thousands of streams in the span of days. Between her own self-released recordings and her three EPs on independent London label Dirty Hit, Patched Up, Loveworm, and Space Cadet, her songs have amassed millions of streams since then. Rolling Stone, Vogue, Dazed, The Fader, i-D, Pigeons and Planes, Noisey, NME, Time Out, Paper, Nylon, Time Out, The New Yorker, and The Guardian are just a few of the publications that have supported her.

Bea received nominations in 2019 for the BBC Sound of 2020 and the BRITs Rising Star awards. This October 16th, she releases her debut album, Fake It Flowers. Acclaim for Beabadoobee: Vogue calls her "a bedroom-wall-of-sound classic... built to echo on infinite repeat"; The Guardian calls her "a DIY sense of rawness, unaffected by industry or concerns over streaming algorithms"; The FADER calls her "intimate, close confessional vocals matched with stargazy guitars... a voice for restless teens the world over"; Pigeons and Planes calls her "galaxy-class starry-eyed swoon-pop."
